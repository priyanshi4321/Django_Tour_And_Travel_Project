Singapore

"Exploring hidden gems in Singapore."

Welcome to Singapore!
Singapore may be small compared to its 
Asian neighbors, but there's a lot
packed into this city-state. 
Between the incredible food markets and 
high-end restaurants, gleaming skyline 
and cultural wealth, your main problem 
will be knowing where to start.
Must See Places in Singapore

Singapore Botanic Gardens

This national park is open daily and features beautiful lakes, animals, flowers and plants, including one of the region's first rubber tree orchards.

Cloud Forest

The cloud forest dome has replicated the cool moist conditions found in tropical mountain regions between 3000 ft

National Orchid Garden

Within the grounds of the Singapore Botanic Gardens, a UNESCO Heritage site, is the National Orchid Garden

Marina Bay

Marina Bay was transformed into a mix of uses for this area, including commercial, residential, hotel.

Chinatown

For a fascinating peek into Singapore’s Chinese culture and history, Chinatown is good place to start.

Merlion Park

Standing at 8.6 metres high and weighing 70 tonnes.

Famous Cuisine in Singapore

Hainanese chicken rice

Steamed chicken served with rice cooked in chicken stock. This all-time favourite dish makes for a quick, fulfilling lunch. The quality of chicken stock is crucial to this dish.

Chilli crab

Hard-shell crabs cooked in semi-thick gravy with a tomato chilli base. The steamed crabs are partially cracked, then lightly stir-fried in a paste comprising of chilli sauce, ketchup and eggs. Despite its name, chilli crab is not all that spicy

Laksa

Rice noodles in spicy coconut curry soup with shrimp, fish cakes, egg and chicken meat – a cross between Chinese and Malay cuisine. Laksa also has many variants.

Char kuay teow

Broad white noodles fried with black Soya sauce, bean sprouts, fish cake, clams and Chinese sausage. You will find this dish in hawker centres and restaurants.

Satay

Skewered grilled meat served with rice cake (ketupat), peanut sauce and cucumber-chili relish. This popular side dish makes an excellent starter or party platter. It has a strong turmeric scent and flavour, as this spice is the key marinade ingredient.

Oyster omelette

An egg omelette mixed with flour and fried with a generous helping of small oysters garnished with coriander leaves, crispy bean sprouts, and a sweet, spicy sauce.

Kaya toast

Kaya is a sweet coconut egg jam spread generously over toasted bread. Eat the toast with half-boiled eggs and add a dash of black soya sauce for extra taste!

Nasi lemak

Nasi lemak is a hearty meal comprising coconut rice, a slice of omelette, anchovies, a slice of cucumber and some chilli paste, uniquely packed in brown paper or banana leaf.

Best Places To Shop

Marina Bay

All of these stores here are great places for both buying high-end brands and indulging in offbeat Singapore shopping.

Chinatown

It is certainly a great place to buy spices, souvenirs, and clothes since all the shops across the road sell it at throwaway prices.

Little India

The most colourful and vibrant place for shopping in Singapore, Little India is a haven for every tourist looking to shop his or her heart out.
