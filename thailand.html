Must See Places in Thailand

The Grand Palace

The architecture and intricate detail of the palace is a testimony to the craftsmanship and creativity of Thai people.

Pattaya Beach

If you want to experience the true beauty of Pattaya, then you need to look beyond all the glittering lights of the city, the pubs, and the clubs.

Phang Nga Bay

Marvel at the stunning beauty of nature at Phang Nga Bay, and get lost in the mesmerizing views of the limestone cliffs protruding out of the turquoise waters.

Krabi Islands

Krabi is filled with lots of pristine islands, so you can enjoy a full-day doing island hopping on a traditional long-tailed boat.

Railay Beach

Railay beach is more like a small peninsula with cluster of white sand beaches that feature soaring limestone cliffs, viewpoints and caves.

Krabi Town

When you travel to Krabi, you will love to visit popular tourist spots and enjoy the local culture. Amongst other Things to do in Krabi , you can surely explore some of the best things to do in Krabi to make your trip a fulfilling one.

Famous Cuisine in Thailand

Stir-Fried Noodles

Thailand’s noodle dishes were introduced by Chinese immigrants and arguably, Pad Thai ranks as the most celebrated all. This signature dish is a great go-to introduction to Thai food as it’s not too spicy.

Hot & Sour Shrimp Soup

Hailing from the central region, the unique flavors of this piquant clear soup derives from the marriage of fragrant lemongrass, shallots, fish sauce, galangal, mushrooms, kaffir lime leaves, lime juice and red chili peppers.

Red Curry

One of Thailand’s most common curry varieties, red curries are a happy medium: generally spicier than yellow curries but less so than the green varieties.

Green Curry

Originating from Central Thailand, green curry is the spiciest of the Thai ‘holy trilogy’ and arguably, most familiar world-wide.

Thai Coconut Curry Noodle Soup

Noodle soups are a popular street food dish, served any time of day or night across Thailand. One of the best loved varieties is Khao Soi (or Soy), a signature Northern Thailand dish, especially revered in Chiang Mai.

Thai Fried Rice

This Thai fried rice dish is a classic, go-to favorite for locals, served anytime, but especially for lunch. Khao Pad is made with either chicken, pork, beef, seafood or tofu, together with eggs, onions, garlic, fish sauce, fresh herbs and tomatoes or other vegetables

Spicy Green Papaya Salad

Arguably Thailand’s best-known salad dishes, Som Tam is thought to have originated in Laos, but now stands as a yummy north-eastern specialty and amongst Thailand’s most beloved dishes.

Lokum

This simple combination of water, starch and sugar – boiled together to produce delicate cubes flavored with rose water, pistachio and other flavors – continues to delight.

Best Places To Shop

Central World

The largest shopping centre in Thailand, Central World hosts multitudes of cafes, restaurants, and stores in its seven-storied complexes.

MBK Center

The MBK Center is a hub for reasonably priced gadgets and fashion items alike. It is one of the oldest malls in the country.

Terminal 21

A delight designed for the travel bugs, Terminal 21 is a one-of-a-kind shopping mall in Thailand that has an airport theme.
